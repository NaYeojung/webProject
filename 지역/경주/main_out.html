Pink muhly colony

Gyeongju Pink Muhly can be seen in the Cheomseongdae area located in Inwang-dong, Gyeongju-si, Gyeongbuk. Every year, around September to November, when pink muhly colonies begin to bloom, many people from all over the country visit.

Gyeongju Najeong

It is a well that preserves the legend of the birth of Park Hyeokgeose, the founder of Silla. In addition to a monument commemorating Park Hyeokgeose, the site of an octagonal building presumed to be the site of a shrine, a well, a fence, an annex building, and a drainage ditch remain.

Queen Seondeok's Tomb

The tomb of Queen Seondeok in Gyeongsu is designated as a historic site, and the surrounding tombs include the tomb of King Sinmun, the tomb of King Hyogong, the tomb of King Sinmu, and the tomb of King Hyo.

Mangwolsa Temple

Mangwolsa Temple is a temple of Wonhyojong of Korean Buddhism located in Bae-dong, Gyeongju-si and is considered an important temple of Wonhyojong. Wonhyojong of Korean Buddhism is one of the 18 Buddhist orders in Korea, with Monk Wonhyo of Silla as its ancestor.

Geumjangdae Waterside Park

Geumjangdae, a towering rock wall along the Hyeongsan River in Gyeongju, has long been called ‘Geumjangnagan (金藏落雁)’ because it is a resting place for wild geese.
