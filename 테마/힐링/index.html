A journey to comfort and heal my tired self

  • 1

    Dongbu Reservoir

    The green rice fields and mountains make you feel like you are in Switzerland. There is a ‘Lovers Tree Photo Zone’ at the entrance of the reservoir, so you can make memories with your friends or lover with the reservoir in the background.

  • 2

    Geoje Cable Car

    From the observatory at the top of Geoje Cable Car, you can enjoy a 360-degree view of Nojasan Mountain and the Archipelago Sea, and the crystal cabin has a glass floor, so you can feel like you are walking through the Nojasan forest path.

  • 3

    Eobanga

    Eobanga's Eobanga refers to a village that catches fish, and Eobanga serves fresh raw fish, seafood, healthy mixed raw fish raw fish made with kelp enzyme, and yellow plum extract, and raw raw fish with thin ice.

  • 4

    Forest Sound Park

    Forest Sound Park is a rural relaxation park that combines animals, plants, and therapy. There is an insect and shiitake mushroom experience center and an acorn playground, and the animal experience center includes a sheep and rabbit feeding experience, making it an experiential space for family visitors.

Gwangju-Jeonnam travel course
to experience both art and history

  • 1

    National Asia Culture Center

    It is equipped with facilities as a hub for culture and arts, including the Democratic and Peace Exchange Center, Children's Cultural Center, Cultural Creation Center, Cultural Information Center, and Art Theater, and has many innovative spaces such as the Creative Production Center and Library Park.

  • 2

    Dongmyeong-dong Cafe Street

    From the 00s to the 90s, Dongmyeong-dong was a neighborhood where the wealthy lived together, but as the residential culture shifted to apartments, academies were concentrated in Dongmyeong-dong, and cafes began to open for mothers who were busy educating their children. As these cafes gradually pursued unique exteriors, interiors, and tastes of drinks, cafes of various styles were established, creating the current Dongmyeong-dong Cafe Street.

  • 3

    Baekun-dong Garden

    The 12 scenic spots suggested by Jeong Yak-yong remain intact, making it the highlight of Korean traditional gardens. It is a place where scholars of the Joseon Dynasty exchanged culture and enjoyed fine arts, and also where Jeong Yak-yong, Zen Master Choeui, and Lee Si-heon made tea. It has a record of being passed down and enjoyed, adding value to the fact that it has become the cradle of our country's tea culture.

A 2-day, 1-night course in Namwon that is beautiful
both during the day and at night.

  • 1

    Namwon Adamwon

    Managed with the know-how of Korea Landscaping, it was reborn as a beautiful garden in August 2018. Along with the unexplored scenery at the foot of Jiri Mountain, it provides visitors with a place of healing and is becoming one of Namwon's leading tourist destinations. The cafe located inside sells brunch menus, drinks, and bakery items. Jukyeonji, where lotus flowers bloom, is famous as Adamwon's most loved trail and photo zone.

  • 2

    Namwon City Kim Byeong-jong Art Museum

    Namwon City Kim Byeong-jong Art Museum, located within Chunhyang Theme Park in Namwon, Jeollabuk-do, is a public art museum directly operated by Namwon City. The foundation for the collection was laid when Kim Byeong-jong, a Korean painter from Namwon, donated a large number of his representative works to Namwon City. This place, humbly bowing down towards Jiri Mountain, called ‘Mother Mountain’, is home to nature, art, and literature.

  • 3

    Gwanghalluwon

    In the first year of King Sejong of the Joseon Dynasty (1419), Hwang Hui built a pavilion and enjoyed landscapes. In 1444, Jeong In-ji, a governor of Jeolla Province, became intoxicated with the beautiful scenery and called this place Gwanghancheongheobu in the Moon Palace, where the beauty of the moon, Hang-ah, lived. It was then named 'Gwanghallu'. . It was. It is also famous as the place where Lee Doryeong and Chunhyang met in the novel 『Chunhyangjeon』, and the Chunhyang Festival is held every year on Dano Festival, May 5th in the lunar calendar.

  • 4

    hornbeam forest

    The hornbeam forest, which won the grand prize at the ‘1st Beautiful Forest Contest’, is an artificial forest created about 200 years ago, and about 100 trees come together to form a dense forest. The hornbeam tree is called a muscular tree because its trunk is strong, and the temperature is always maintained around 15℃, so even in the summer it feels cool as if the air conditioner was turned on. In addition, it is located at the foot of ‘Unbong’ on Jiri Mountain, so the scenery is also outstanding.
